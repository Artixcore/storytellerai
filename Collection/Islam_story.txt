chapter-1-Chapter 1: Introduction to Islam 

Islam is a monotheistic religion originating in the 7th century CE in the Arabian Peninsula. It proclaims belief in one God, Allah, and his final prophet, Mohammed. It includes teachings on morality, justice, and life after death, with core practices known as the Five Pillars of Islam.



chapter-2-Chapter 2: Beliefs and Practices in Islam 

Islam is a monotheistic religion, emphasizing the belief in one God, Allah. Core practices include five pillars: faith declaration, prayer five times daily, giving alms to the poor, fasting during Ramadan, and a pilgrimage to Mecca. It nurtures a strong sense of community, morality, and adherence to God's commands.
